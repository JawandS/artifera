## Challenge Tracks

Teams choose **one** of three tracks:

### Track 1 — AI's Future in Higher Education *(Artifera's track)*
**Focus areas:** Smart Learning · Accessibility · Ethics · Student Well-Being

**Challenge question:**
> How can universities leverage AI for learning and accessibility without compromising creativity?

**Full framing:** AI continues to shape how learning is done both inside and outside the classroom. From personalized study tools to automated feedback on exams and assignments, AI has become embedded in academic life. With its promises of efficiency, concerns are being raised about its accuracy, fairness, and the impact it has on student well-being. Poorly designed or unevenly distributed AI can widen equity gaps in education, lead to increases in stress, and weaken trust in the learning process.

**Core challenge:** What can be done to help universities use AI to enhance learning, accessibility, and mental well-being, without replacing human learning, creativity, and judgment? How can AI be used to deepen understanding while being equitable and a positive force for both educators and students?

## Strategic Notes for the Presentation

- **Track 1 asks specifically about equity and not replacing human judgment** — Artifera directly addresses both. The randomized grant draw is the equity answer; the teacher-verified, teacher-created model preserves human authorship.
- **The "execution" requirement is where many teams are weak** — Artifera has a concrete three-phase roadmap with a named pilot partner (William & Mary), a clear funding model, and phased sponsor integration.
- **Optional demo opportunity** — A React landing page or mockup of the marketplace UI could serve as the concept demo, differentiating the submission from pure slide decks.
- **The keynote speaker is Brandon Hennessey from the W&M Entrepreneurship Hub** — the pilot starting at William & Mary is directly aligned with the event's institutional context, which strengthens credibility.
